# Product Marketing Context: Gentle Care Nursing

*Last updated: 2026-04-28*

> **Voice rules (binding — applied sitewide):**
> - Do NOT use "boutique". Use instead: personalised care, quality-focused care, small caseload, clinician-led care, consistent person-centred support, relationship-based care.
> - Do NOT use "24-hour response guarantee". Use: "We aim to respond within 24 hours" or "Prompt response within business hours, with urgent referrals prioritised".
> - Do NOT use specific years/duration claims ("since 2014", "X+ years operating"). Use: "Led by a care professional with over 10 years of hands-on experience in disability, aged care and in-home support."
> - DVA wording must be: "DVA Contracted Community Nursing Provider" or "Contracted to provide DVA Community Nursing services". Never "DVA Registered Provider" or "DVA Approved".
> - 24/7: "We can provide overnight support and extended hours depending on care needs, funding and staff availability."
> - Tone: professional, accurate, healthcare-appropriate. NOT commercial. NOT salesy.

## Product Overview
**One-liner:** Personalised, clinician-led in-home nursing and disability support in Sydney, with a deliberately small caseload.
**What it does:** Provides personalised, high-quality clinical care and personal support to NDIS participants, veterans (DVA), and older Australians at home.
**Product category:** In-Home Care / Community Nursing
**Product type:** Service
**Business model:** Government-funded (NDIS, DVA Community Nursing, Support at Home) and private fee-for-service.

## Target Audience
**Target companies:** NDIS Support Coordination agencies, Hospital Discharge Units, Allied Health clinics.
**Decision-makers:** Support Coordinators, Families/Carers, Hospital Case Managers.
**Primary use case:** Families and coordinators needing a reliable, responsive, quality-focused clinical provider in Sydney.
**Jobs to be done:**
- Ensure safe transition from hospital to home.
- Maintain independence for NDIS participants with complex clinical needs.
- Provide peace of mind for families caring for an elderly parent.

## Personas
| Persona | Cares about | Challenge | Value we promise |
|---------|-------------|-----------|------------------|
| **Support Coordinator** | Reliability & responsiveness | Providers that don't respond or fail to deliver care. | We aim to respond within 24 hours, with urgent referrals prioritised, and clinician-led care delivery. |
| **Family Member** | Peace of mind & trust | Stress of managing care for a loved one. | Relationship-based care from a small, consistent team that learns routines and protects dignity. |
| **Hospital Planner** | Speed & safety | Delayed discharge due to lack of home support. | Prompt response within business hours, urgent post-hospital referrals prioritised, safe hospital-to-home coordination. |

## Problems & Pain Points
**Core problem:** The "care factory" model (high volume, low quality, poor communication).
**Why alternatives fall short:**
- Long wait times for enquiries.
- Rushed visits with high staff turnover.
- Lack of clinical oversight by registered nurses.
**What it costs them:** Safety risks, family stress, and participant regression.
**Emotional tension:** Fear of loved ones being unsafe; frustration with unresponsive systems.

## Competitive Landscape
**Direct:** Large national NDIS/Aged Care providers — fall short because they prioritise volume over personalised connection.
**Secondary:** Small independent support workers — fall short because they lack registered nurse oversight and clinical governance.
**Indirect:** Residential aged care — falls short because clients prefer independence and dignity at home.

## Differentiation
**Key differentiators:**
- **Quality over volume** — we take fewer clients so each person gets more dedicated, attentive care.
- **Clinician-led** — led by a care professional with over 10 years of hands-on experience in disability, aged care and in-home support; clinical care delivered or supervised by AHPRA-registered nurses.
- **Prompt, honest response** — we aim to respond within 24 hours during business hours, with urgent referrals prioritised, and we are upfront about timing.
**How we do it differently:** Small, consistent care team with relationship-based matching rather than rotating agency staff.
**Why that's better:** More consistent care, stronger continuity, and clearer communication for families and coordinators.
**Why customers choose us:** They want a provider that actually listens, follows through, and treats people as people.

## Switching Dynamics
**Push:** Frustration with "faceless" large providers and lack of consistency.
**Pull:** The promise of a personal, professional nurse who knows your name and shows up.
**Habit:** "It's easier to stay with the current provider even if they are 'okay'."
**Anxiety:** "Will a smaller provider have the clinical depth we need?"

## Customer Language
**How they describe the problem:**
- "I just want someone to listen."
- "The big companies just treat us like a number."
- "I can never get anyone on the phone."
**How they describe us:**
- "They actually care."
- "They were so quick to respond."
**Words to use:** Compassionate, responsive, personalised, clinician-led, relationship-based, quality-focused, consistent, person-centred, dignity, independence, small caseload.
**Words to avoid:** Boutique, premium, hyper-personalised, guarantee/guaranteed (response), volume, automation, synergy, revolutionary.
