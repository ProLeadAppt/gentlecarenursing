# Product Marketing Context: Gentle Care Nursing

*Last updated: 2026-03-18*

## Product Overview
**One-liner:** Boutique, clinician-led in-home nursing and disability support in Sydney.
**What it does:** Provides personalized, high-quality clinical care and personal support to NDIS participants, veterans (DVA), and older Australians at home.
**Product category:** In-Home Care / Community Nursing
**Product type:** Service
**Business model:** Government-funded (NDIS, DVA, My Aged Care) and Private Fee-for-Service.

## Target Audience
**Target companies:** NDIS Support Coordination agencies, Hospital Discharge Units, Allied Health clinics.
**Decision-makers:** Support Coordinators, Families/Carers, Hospital Case Managers.
**Primary use case:** Families and coordinators needing a reliable, responsive, and high-quality clinical provider in Sydney.
**Jobs to be done:**
- Ensure safe transition from hospital to home.
- Maintain independence for NDIS participants with complex clinical needs.
- Provide peace of mind for families caring for an elderly parent.

## Personas
| Persona | Cares about | Challenge | Value we promise |
|---------|-------------|-----------|------------------|
| **Support Coordinator** | Reliability & Responsiveness | Providers that don't respond or fail to deliver care. | 24-hour response guarantee & Clinical excellence. |
| **Family Member** | Peace of Mind & Trust | Stress of managing care for a loved one. | Boutique care where your loved one is treated like family. |
| **Hospital Planner** | Speed & Safety | Delayed discharge due to lack of home support. | Rapid turnaround and safe hospital-to-home coordination. |

## Problems & Pain Points
**Core problem:** The "Care Factory" model (high volume, low quality, poor communication).
**Why alternatives fall short:**
- Long wait times for enquiries.
- Rushed visits with high staff turnover.
- Lack of clinical oversight by Registered Nurses.
**What it costs them:** Safety risks, family stress, and participant regression.
**Emotional tension:** Fear of loved ones being unsafe; frustration with unresponsive systems.

## Competitive Landscape
**Direct:** Large national NDIS/Aged Care providers — fall short because they prioritize volume over personalized connection.
**Secondary:** Small independent support workers — fall short because they lack Registered Nurse oversight and clinical governance.
**Indirect:** Residential Aged Care — falls short because clients prefer independence and dignity at home.

## Differentiation
**Key differentiators:**
- **Quality Over Volume**: We take fewer clients to give more dedicated care.
- **Clinician-Led**: Owned and operated by experienced Registered Nurses.
- **24-Hour Guarantee**: Guaranteed response to all enquiries within one business day.
**How we do it differently:** Boutique focus with a small, highly-skilled team.
**Why that's better:** More consistent care, better outcomes, and stronger relationships.
**Why customers choose us:** They want a provider that actually *cares* and *responds*.

## Switching Dynamics
**Push:** Frustration with "faceless" large providers and lack of consistency.
**Pull:** The promise of a personal, professional nurse who knows your name.
**Habit:** "It's easier to stay with the current provider even if they are 'okay'."
**Anxiety:** "Will a smaller provider have the clinical depth we need?"
